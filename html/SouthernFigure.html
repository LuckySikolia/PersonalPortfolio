SOUTHERN FIGURE

Problem Statement

Southern Figure is a faux luxury motorcycle company that emphasizes customizability and caters to a female target audience. They want a website that connects luxury motorcycle enthusiasts to a brand that allows them to redefine the riding experience with its unparalleled blend of premium motorcycles.

Design Process

01. Research
Competitor Analysis

The initial stage of my design process involved conducting comprehensive research, primarily centered around competitive analysis. This research enabled me to gain a deep understanding of the industry landscape, spot significant trends, and identify best practices.

Platforms like MotorHunt and CycleTrader offer a space for motorcycle enthusiasts to peruse and buy motorcycles but lack the luxury factor that Southern Figure aims to embody. Conversely, Ducati and Harley-Davidson showcase remarkable design and user interfaces, effectively conveying their brand identities through engaging visuals and compelling content. While these websites excel in their e-commerce features, I saw they fall short in establishing a memorable and captivating brand image that connects with Southern Figure's target audience of female riders.

Secondary Research

This involved exploring motorcycle-centered communities on platforms such as Reddit and online blog posts. I identified common themes, trends, and pain points that I could address in the website design. I learned that many women in the motorcycle community felt that they were not adequately represented by existing brands.

Research Summary

Southern Figure's unique proposition lies in seamlessly marrying functionality with a luxurious brand identity that deeply resonates with female riders. By prioritizing elegance, sophistication, and a sense of empowerment, Southern Figure aims to carve out a distinctive presence in the motorcycle industry, catering to the underserved niche of female motorcycle enthusiasts.


02. Analysis
Insights from research

In this stage I merged insights from the research phase, formulated the design objectives, pinpointed essential features, and addressed user concerns. This served as the foundation of a comprehensive roadmap that adhered to Southern Figure's brand.

User Pain Points
  • The underrepresentation of female riders in the motorcycle industry.
  • Difficulty in accessing customer service.
  • Overwhelming number of questions during the purchase process for first-time buyers.
Primary Design Objectives
  • Convey a sense of luxury and sophistication that holds particular allure for discerning female riders.
  • Forge a user-friendly and intuitive e-commerce environment that streamlines the motorcycle purchasing process.
  • Articulate Southern Figure's unwavering commitment to quality, personalization, and empowerment.
Key Features
  • A captivating hero section that not only portrays the brand's identity but also spotlights its unique selling points
  • A seamlessly organized shop page replete with top-tier imagery, comprehensive product descriptions, and tailored product recommendations.
Focus Design Principles

03. Ideate
Insights from research

This stage focused on the creation of a site map, which served as a blueprint for the overall structure and organisation of the website. The site map shows the hierarchy of pages, their interrelationships, and the navigation paths linking them together.

Concurrently, I developed a user flow, focusing on one of the pivotal functions—the shopping experience. It outlines the steps a user would follow to browse, select, and ultimately purchase a motorcycle.

04. Prototyping
UI Design

Guided by the site map and user flows I created a low-fidelity sketch and a high-fidelity wireframe to visualize the layout, content arrangement, and interactive elements of the website.

The low-fidelity sketch served as a rapid ideation tool, enabling me to experiment with diverse layouts and explore design possibilities without being mired in intricate details. My focus was on capturing the overarching structure and fundamental components of each page, conveyed through simple shapes and annotations.

Building upon the foundation laid by the low-fidelity sketch, I developed a high-fidelity wireframe which integrated more refined visual elements, encompassing typography, color palettes, and iconography.

The prototyping stage played a pivotal role in bridging the divide between abstract ideas and tangible design elements.

05. User Testing
User Feedback
  • One concern was the absence of a search bar, leading to difficulties for users in quickly locating the specific motorcycle they were interested in. To rectify this, I introduced a prominently placed search bar on the homepage and throughout the website.
  • Another issue was the absence of an "about" page, limiting users' opportunities to delve into Southern Figure's brand history, values, and dedication to quality and customization. In response, I developed a comprehensive about page that showcased the company's narrative, its mission to empower female riders, and its commitment to delivering exceptional customer service.
  • Third issue was a desire for more customization options . To enhance the user experience, I integrated a customization section into the checkout flow, enabling users to communicate their specific preferences directly to the company. This customization feature not only personalized the purchasing experience but also underscored Southern Figure's dedication to meeting the unique requirements of its discerning clientele.
Final thought

In embarking on this project, I encountered several challenges that ultimately contributed to my learning as a designer.

Firstly, as my first solo ux design endeavor, the magnitude of decisions to be made was at times overwhelming. However, I welcomed this challenge as it provided me with the valuable opportunity to learn how to prioritize and tackle the most critical issues effectively.

Secondly, the self-imposed time constraint of completing the project within a month turned out to be an understatement. More time was required, particularly for refining screen designs and comprehensive testing.

Lastly, the limited pool of users available for testing posed a significant challenge.

Looking ahead to future self-initiated projects, I intend to seek involvement in UX communities where I can access a broader range of testers and tap into the insights of potential users and UX experts.
